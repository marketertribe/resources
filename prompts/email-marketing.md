# AI Email Marketing Prompts

Copy-paste these prompts into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant to generate high-converting email copy.

## Welcome Sequence (5 emails)

### Email 1: Welcome & Brand Story
```
Write a welcome email for [BRAND]. The subscriber just signed up for [LEAD MAGNET].
Tone: Warm, personal, conversational.
Include: Thank them, deliver the lead magnet, share a brief origin story, set expectations for what's coming.
Length: 200-300 words.
```

### Email 2: Quick Win
```
Write the second email in a welcome sequence for [BRAND].
Goal: Give the subscriber an immediate, actionable quick win related to [TOPIC].
Include: One specific tip they can implement in under 10 minutes.
Tone: Helpful, encouraging.
Length: 150-250 words.
```

### Email 3: Social Proof
```
Write email 3 of a welcome sequence for [BRAND].
Goal: Build credibility through social proof.
Include: 2-3 customer success stories or testimonials related to [PRODUCT/SERVICE].
Tone: Inspirational but authentic. No hype.
Length: 200-300 words.
```

### Email 4: Objection Handling
```
Write email 4 of a welcome sequence for [BRAND].
Goal: Address the top 3 objections people have about [PRODUCT/SERVICE].
Format: FAQ style with conversational answers.
Tone: Understanding, empathetic, honest.
Length: 250-350 words.
```

### Email 5: Soft Pitch
```
Write the final email in a 5-part welcome sequence for [BRAND].
Goal: Soft pitch for [PRODUCT/SERVICE] at [PRICE].
Include: Recap the value they've received, paint a picture of transformation, clear CTA.
Tone: Confident but not pushy. Zero hype language.
Length: 200-300 words.
```

## Re-Engagement Sequence

### Win-Back Email
```
Write a re-engagement email for subscribers who haven't opened in 60+ days.
Brand: [BRAND] in the [INDUSTRY] space.
Include: Acknowledge the silence, remind them why they signed up, offer something valuable to re-engage.
Subject line options: Provide 5 curiosity-driven subject lines.
Tone: Friendly, no guilt. Make them feel welcome back.
```

### Last Chance / Sunset Email
```
Write a final "sunset" email for inactive subscribers of [BRAND].
Goal: Either re-engage them or let them go gracefully.
Include: Clear unsubscribe option, one compelling reason to stay, what they'll miss.
Tone: Respectful and direct.
```

## Newsletter Prompts

### Weekly Digest
```
Write a weekly marketing digest email for [BRAND].
Topics to cover: [TOPIC 1], [TOPIC 2], [TOPIC 3].
Format: Brief intro, 3 sections with headers, each 2-3 sentences with a takeaway.
Include: One actionable tip at the end.
Tone: Knowledgeable, casual, like a smart friend sharing what they learned this week.
```
