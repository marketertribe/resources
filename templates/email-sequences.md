# Email Sequence Templates

Plug-and-play email sequence structures for common marketing scenarios.

## Welcome Sequence (5 Emails)

| Email | Day | Goal | Subject Line Formula |
|-------|-----|------|---------------------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Deliver lead magnet + introduce brand | "Here's your [thing]" |
| 2 | Day 1 | Quick win / immediate value | "Try this in the next 10 minutes" |
| 3 | Day 3 | Social proof + credibility | "How [Name] got [Result]" |
| 4 | Day 5 | Overcome objections | "The #1 question we get" |
| 5 | Day 7 | Soft pitch | "Ready for the next step?" |

## Launch Sequence (7 Emails)

| Email | Day | Goal | Subject Line Formula |
|-------|-----|------|---------------------|
| 1 | Day -3 | Seed the launch | "Something's coming..." |
| 2 | Day -1 | Build anticipation | "Tomorrow changes everything" |
| 3 | Day 0 | Launch announcement | "It's here: [Product Name]" |
| 4 | Day 1 | Features + benefits deep dive | "Here's exactly what you get" |
| 5 | Day 3 | Social proof / case study | "[Name] already got results" |
| 6 | Day 5 | FAQ / objection handling | "Your questions, answered" |
| 7 | Day 7 | Final call / urgency | "Last chance — doors close tonight" |

## Nurture Sequence (Ongoing)

| Week | Content Type | Example |
|------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Educational tip | "3 things I'd do differently" |
| 2 | Behind the scenes | "What we're working on" |
| 3 | Curated resources | "Best things I read this week" |
| 4 | Soft pitch | "In case you missed it" |
